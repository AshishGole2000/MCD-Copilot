# Speed Dating Interviews – Social Media Command Centre

## Interview 1: Shift Supervisor (Restaurant Operations)

**Location:** McDonald’s – Champaign, IL  
**Objective:** Understand operational bottlenecks that lead to negative reviews and identify gaps between internal operations and online feedback.

---

### Q1. What operational issues cause the most complaints?

**Answer:** During lunch rush the biggest problem is the drive-thru queue getting too long. When the kitchen is overwhelmed, mistakes happen and orders sometimes go out incomplete.

---

### Q2. Are online reviews usually accurate?

**Answer:** Sometimes they are, but not always. A single delay during peak hours can lead to a very harsh review even if the rest of the shift ran smoothly.

---

### Q3. What kind of feedback would be most helpful for managers?

**Answer:** If reviews could automatically highlight patterns like repeated wait time complaints or missing items, that would make it easier to identify where the workflow is breaking down.

---

### Q4. Do customers complain about different things online vs in-store?

**Answer:** Yes. In person they usually mention wait time. Online they often complain about food temperature or accuracy because they notice it after leaving.

---

### Q5. If AI could summarize reviews automatically, what should it focus on?

**Answer:** Categorizing issues like drive-thru delays, order mistakes, and food temperature problems. That would help managers prioritize fixes faster.

# Interview 2: Frequent Customer

**Location:** Champaign, IL  
**Objective:** Understand positive experience drivers and why customers sometimes leave mixed reviews.

---

### Q1. Why do you choose McDonald’s?

**Answer:** Mainly convenience. It’s fast and predictable, especially when I’m busy or grabbing something quickly between classes.

---

### Q2. What frustrates you the most?

**Answer:** Waiting longer than expected. If I’m choosing fast food, it’s usually because I don’t have much time.

---

### Q3. Have you ever left a mixed review?

**Answer:** Yes. Sometimes the food is fine but the wait was long. I might still give 4 stars but mention the delay in the text.

---

### Q4. Do star ratings capture your experience well?

**Answer:** Not really. A rating is quick to give, but the text explains the real issue.

---

### Q5. What would improve your experience?

**Answer:** Better updates on mobile orders. If there’s a delay, I’d rather know earlier than wait without any information.

---

# Interview 3: Dissatisfied Customer

**Location:** Champaign, IL  
**Objective:** Understand drivers of negative sentiment and how customers express dissatisfaction online.

---

### Q1. What usually leads you to leave a negative review?

**Answer:** When the order is wrong or missing items. That’s frustrating because you don’t always notice until you’re already home.

---

### Q2. Do you ever use sarcasm in reviews?

**Answer:** Yes. Sometimes people say things like *“Amazing service if you enjoy waiting 20 minutes.”* It’s a way to show frustration.

---

### Q3. Do ratings reflect the real issue?

**Answer:** Not always. A low rating might be because of something specific like cold food, but the number alone doesn’t explain that.

---

### Q4. Would you trust automated summaries of reviews?

**Answer:** If they highlighted the real issue like long wait times or incorrect orders, that would probably help managers fix things faster.

---

### Q5. What would make you reconsider returning?

**Answer:** More consistent service. If orders were accurate and wait times improved, I’d be more willing to go back.

# Key Insights from Speed Dating Interviews

Common themes identified across interviews:

- **Wait time sensitivity during peak hours**
- **Order accuracy issues when the store is busy**
- **Mismatch between star ratings and review text**
- **Sarcasm present in negative reviews**
- **Mixed sentiment within single experiences**

Operational categories useful for AI tagging:

- Wait Time  
- Order Accuracy  
- Food Temperature / Quality  
- Cleanliness  
- Staff Behavior  
- Mobile Order Communication

---

### Implication for the Social Media Command Centre

These interviews highlight that customer reviews often contain **specific operational signals that simple star ratings cannot capture**.  

An AI-driven command centre could help managers by automatically categorizing feedback into operational issues (e.g., wait time, order accuracy, food temperature) and identifying recurring patterns across reviews.
