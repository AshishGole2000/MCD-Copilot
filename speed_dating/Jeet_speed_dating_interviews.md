## Interview 1: Employee  
Location: McDonald's - 616 E Green St, Champaign, IL, US  

### Objective  
Understand operational pain points, feedback gaps, and sentiment mismatches from a frontline employee perspective.

### Q1. What are the most common customer complaints here?  
**Answer:** Mostly long wait times during rush hours and occasional wrong orders when it gets too busy.

### Q2. When do operational issues usually happen?  
**Answer:** Late nights and lunch rush. We get flooded with mobile and in-store orders at the same time.

### Q3. Do online reviews accurately reflect what happens in-store?  
**Answer:** Not always. Some customers are polite in person but very harsh online. Sometimes one small mistake becomes a dramatic review.

### Q4. Do customers use sarcasm in complaints?  
**Answer:** Yes. For example, someone wrote, “Best fast food experience if you enjoy waiting 20 minutes.” They were clearly upset about delay.

### Q5. If AI could flag operational issues automatically, what would help most?  
**Answer:** Categorizing complaints into wait time, order accuracy, and food temperature. That would help management fix real bottlenecks faster.

## Interview 2: McDonald's Lover (Frequent Customer)

### Objective  
Understand positive sentiment drivers and subtle friction points that ratings alone may not capture.

### Q1. Why do you enjoy McDonald's?  
**Answer:** It’s consistent, affordable, and quick. I know exactly what I’m getting every time.

### Q2. What matters more to you, speed or food quality?  
**Answer:** Speed during weekdays. Quality matters more when I’m eating casually.

### Q3. Have you ever had a mixed experience?  
**Answer:** Yes. Food was good but the dining area wasn’t very clean once. I still rated it 4 stars.

### Q4. Would you leave a negative review for one bad experience?  
**Answer:** Only if it was repeated. One bad day can happen.

### Q5. What could make your experience even better?  
**Answer:** Faster pickup for mobile orders and better communication when delays happen.

## Interview 3: McDonald's Hater (Disappointed Customer)

### Objective  
Identify operational weaknesses and gaps between star ratings and actual dissatisfaction drivers.

### Q1. Why don’t you like McDonald's?  
**Answer:** It feels inconsistent. Sometimes the food is cold or rushed.

### Q2. What frustrates you the most?  
**Answer:** Waiting too long for something labeled “fast food.”

### Q3. Have you ever left a negative review?  
**Answer:** Yes. I mentioned slow service and incorrect order.

### Q4. Do star ratings capture your frustration fully?  
**Answer:** Not really. I might give 2 stars, but the real issue is specific like cold fries or wrong item.

### Q5. What would make you reconsider?  
**Answer:** More consistent quality control and accurate orders every time.

---

## Key Themes Identified

- Wait time sensitivity during peak hours  
- Order accuracy issues during rush periods  
- Rating-text mismatches  
- Sarcasm present in negative reviews  
- Mixed sentiment within single experiences  
- Operational categories useful for AI tagging:
  - Wait Time  
  - Order Accuracy  
  - Food Temperature  
  - Cleanliness  
  - Staff Behavior
